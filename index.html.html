Did you know? Several programs can dramatically reduce or eliminate your prescription costs — even brand-name medications.
A federal program that helps pay Medicare Part D costs including premiums, deductibles, and co-pays. Apply through Social Security at (800) 772-1213 or ssa.gov.
SHINE (Serving Health Insurance Needs of Elders) provides FREE unbiased Medicare counseling by trained volunteers across Florida. Call the Elder Helpline: (800) 963-5337.
Free discount cards can reduce costs by up to 80% at most pharmacies. Visit GoodRx.com or ask your pharmacist about programs available.
Most major drug manufacturers offer free or reduced-cost medicines to qualifying patients. Visit NeedyMeds.org to search by medication name.

Medicare can be confusing. Free help is available to explain your options and find programs you qualify for.
  • Part A – Hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice
  • Part B – Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive screenings
  • Part C (Medicare Advantage) – Private plans combining A & B
  • Part D – Prescription drug coverage
  • Medigap – Supplemental insurance to cover gaps
Call the Elder Helpline at (800) 963-5337 to schedule a free appointment with a certified SHINE counselor who can review your coverage and help lower your costs.
If your income is limited, you may qualify for Medicaid, which covers services Medicare does not, including long-term care. Apply at ACCESS Florida or call (866) 762-2237.

⚠️ Warning: Older adults are frequently targeted by scammers. Knowing the warning signs protects you and your savings.
  • Medicare or Social Security imposter phone calls
  • "Grandparent scams" — pretending to be a grandchild in trouble
  • Lottery or prize scams asking for upfront fees
  • Romance scams on dating websites or social media
  • Tech support scams claiming your computer has a virus
  • Home repair scams after a storm or disaster
Do not be embarrassed — scams are designed to fool smart people.

• AARP Fraud Helpline: (877) 908-3360
• Florida Attorney General: (866) 966-7226
• FTC: reportfraud.ftc.gov
Free alerts, resources, and a helpline to protect yourself. Visit aarp.org/fraud or call (877) 908-3360.

Home care allows older adults to remain in their own homes while receiving help with daily tasks.
Home care includes help with bathing, dressing, meals, housekeeping, and medication reminders — provided by trained aides in your home. Home health care includes skilled nursing and therapy ordered by a doctor.
Medicare covers skilled home health care (nursing, therapy) when ordered by a doctor and you are homebound. It does not cover non-medical home care. Medicaid may cover personal care aides. Call SHINE at (800) 963-5337 for help.

Living in Southwest Florida means being prepared for hurricanes. Registering for special assistance BEFORE a storm can be lifesaving.
If you have medical needs, mobility issues, or require electricity for medical equipment, register with your county NOW — before a storm.

Lee County: (239) 533-3911
Collier County: (239) 252-3600
  • At least 7 days of medications in original containers
  • Copies of medical records, insurance cards, and ID
  • Water — 1 gallon per person per day for 3–7 days
  • Non-perishable food and a manual can opener
  • Flashlight, batteries, and a portable radio
  • Phone charger and backup battery pack
  • First aid kit and extra eyeglasses
After a disaster call 211 for local recovery resources. Register with FEMA at 1-800-621-3362. American Red Cross: 1-800-733-2767.
